Make sure you offer a variety of colorful vegetable options. Your child won’t eat them, but they will look pretty in the garbage disposal

Let your child help with the cooking. They won’t eat the finished product, but at least it will take twice as long and there will be a huge mess to clean up

Don’t tell your child they have to finish their vegetables in order to get their dessert. According to the experts, this will give your child food issues and make them hate vegetables. Instead, vaguely sputter about healthy eating and then wonder how the hell you will ever get your damned pie if you wait for them to voluntarily eat vegetables

Don’t force them to eat. They will eat when they are hungry. Eventually you may even learn to ignore that gnawing feeling of shame when you send your starving child off to bed

Don’t make them special meals; expect them to eat what you eat. Expect that you will be eating macaroni & cheese four nights a week

The odds of your child liking the same meal twice are roughly the same as your winning the lottery while being eaten by a shark that has been struck by lightning

Don’t make them finish what is on their plate. That’s your job

A cup with a lid will never tip over. Lidless cups will be knocked off the table twice before dinner starts

Always remember that the amount of effort that you put into a meal is inversely proportional to the how much your child will eat
